VTDigger’s guide to the most important bills of 2019

The 2019 session ended in disappointment for Democrats, who failed to pass two of their priorities — minimum wage and paid family leave — and watched another, handgun waiting periods, get vetoed by Gov. Phil Scott.

But the governor also signed into law what may be the widest reaching abortion protections in America in a year when many states are moving in the other directions. And the Legislature passed dozens of other bills that take a step forward on issues like affordable child care, lead testing in schools and opening a path for civil suits over child sexual abuse.

VTDigger compiled a list of the main issues the Senate and the House attempted to address — successfully and unsuccessfully. Here’s the rundown of what they accomplished and what they didn’t, with summaries of the bills or initiatives and vote tallies in the House and Senate.
